BOOK TYPES

Each book published by the author is unique. And yet any book can be attributed to a certain type. There are several such types and it is necessary to know them, because the UDC and LBC classification indices are built on them, which must be used when publishing a book. These indexes play an extremely important role, because with their help books are placed on the shelves of shops and libraries. Let's take a closer look at them.

According to their social purpose, books are divided into educational, popular science, scientific, reference, and fiction. By the nature of their content, they can be divided into historical, mathematical, biological, philosophical, art history, universal and diversified. According to the reader's address, books are divided into two groups - for an adult reader and for children.

The publication of a book included in the first group, intended for adults, is divided into those addressed to: the general reader, i.e., practically for any person who can read; for a wide range of readers - a narrower group compared to the first, limited by some criteria, for example, by age - youth or pensioners, by education - secondary or higher, by interest in a particular topic - cinema or theater; to a narrow circle of readers - the smallest group, united by belonging to the specifics of one topic.

According to the number of readers in each of these groups, books can be mass, popular or narrowly grouped. The reader's address for children can also be divided into several subspecies: up to six years old inclusive - younger and older preschoolers; from seven to ten years old - younger schoolchildren; from eleven to fourteen years old - middle-aged schoolchildren; and older schoolchildren - from fifteen to seventeen years.
